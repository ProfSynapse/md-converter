Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

Your Privacy Matters

We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Document Converter service is designed with privacy as a core principle: uploaded files are encrypted at rest using your unique session key and automatically deleted within 1 hour. We cannot read your files even if we wanted to, and we do not analyze, index, use for training, or permanently store your document content.

Open Source & Transparent: This service is fully open source. You can review the code, verify our security claims, and even self-host it at github.com/ProfSynapse/doc-converter

1. Service Overview

Document Converter is a web-based service provided by Synaptic Labs that allows you to convert Markdown and HTML files to Word (DOCX), PDF, and Google Docs formats. Our service is designed to be simple, secure, and privacy-focused.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Files You Upload

When you use our service, you upload files for conversion. These files are:

  • Encrypted immediately after conversion using Fernet symmetric encryption (AES-128)
  • Encryption key stored only in your browser session - we cannot decrypt your files without your active session
  • Temporarily stored on our servers in isolated, encrypted directories during the download window
  • Automatically deleted within 1 hour of upload via automated background cleanup
  • Used solely for the purpose of converting them to your requested format(s)
  • Never analyzed, indexed, or used for training AI models or any other purpose
  • Never shared with third parties except as required for conversion (see Section 4)
  • Stored with UUID-based filenames and path traversal protection for security
  • Unreadable to us - even with server access, files are encrypted with your unique session key

2.2 Google Account Information (Optional)

If you choose to convert files to Google Docs format, you will be asked to sign in with your Google account. We collect:

  • Basic profile information: Your name, email address, and profile picture
  • OAuth access tokens: Temporary credentials that allow us to create documents in your Google Drive
  • Session data: Stored in encrypted cookies to maintain your signed-in state

Important: We do not store your Google account credentials. OAuth tokens are stored only in your browser session and are automatically cleared when you sign out or close your browser.

2.3 Usage Information

We collect minimal usage data to maintain service quality:

  • Server logs: Timestamps, file sizes, conversion formats requested, error messages
  • Technical information: IP addresses, browser type, operating system (for debugging purposes)
  • Performance metrics: Conversion times, success/failure rates (aggregated data only)

This information is used solely for service operation, debugging, and improvement. We do not use this data for tracking, profiling, or advertising.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information exclusively for the following purposes:

  • File conversion: Processing your uploaded files and converting them to your requested format(s)
  • Google Docs creation: Using your OAuth credentials to create documents in your Google Drive (only if you choose this option)
  • Service operation: Maintaining, monitoring, and improving the service
  • Security: Detecting and preventing abuse, security threats, or technical issues
  • Legal compliance: Complying with legal obligations and enforcing our terms of service

We do not:

  • Sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes
  • Use your files or content for training AI models or any other purpose
  • Track your browsing activity across other websites
  • Use cookies for advertising or analytics (except as required for authentication)

4. Third-Party Services

Our service uses the following third-party technologies to provide conversions:

4.1 Google Services (Optional)

If you choose to convert files to Google Docs:

  • You will authenticate via Google OAuth 2.0
  • We use the Google Docs API and Google Drive API to create documents in your Drive
  • Documents are created directly in your Google Drive account and are owned by you
  • Google's Privacy Policy applies to your use of Google services
  • We request minimal permissions: docs.documents, drive.file (file-level access only), userinfo.profile

4.2 Processing Libraries

We use the following open-source tools for conversion (all processing happens on our servers):

  • Pandoc: For converting Markdown to Word (DOCX) format
  • Playwright: For rendering PDF documents
  • These tools do not transmit your data to external services

4.3 Hosting Provider

Our service is hosted on Railway (railway.app). Their infrastructure provides:

  • Secure data transmission (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Server security and maintenance
  • Railway does not have access to your file contents

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption at rest: All converted files are encrypted using Fernet (AES-128 CBC with HMAC authentication) immediately after creation
  • Session-based encryption keys: Encryption keys are generated per-job and stored only in your browser session - we cannot decrypt your files without your active session
  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS
  • Automatic deletion: Uploaded files and converted documents are automatically deleted within 1 hour via background cleanup thread (runs every 5 minutes)
  • Zero-knowledge architecture: We cannot read your files even with full server access because we don't have your encryption keys
  • Secure file handling: Files are stored in UUID-based isolated directories (e.g., /tmp/converted/a1b2c3d4-...) with path traversal protection
  • Secure deletion: Files are overwritten with random data before deletion to prevent recovery
  • Session security: OAuth tokens and encryption keys are stored only in encrypted, httponly, secure, samesite session cookies
  • Input validation: All uploads are validated for file type, size, UTF-8 encoding, and content safety
  • No persistent storage: Your Google OAuth credentials are never stored in a database - only in your browser session
  • Isolation: Each conversion job runs in its own isolated directory to prevent cross-contamination

While we take reasonable precautions, no internet transmission is 100% secure. You should not upload highly sensitive or confidential documents without understanding the inherent risks of internet-based services.

6. Data Retention

  • Uploaded files: Encrypted immediately after conversion, deleted within 1 hour
  • Converted files: Encrypted on disk, deleted within 1 hour of creation via automated background cleanup
  • Encryption keys: Stored only in your browser session; automatically destroyed when session expires (typically 1-2 hours of inactivity)
  • OAuth tokens: Stored only in your browser session; cleared when you sign out or close your browser
  • Server logs: Retained for up to 30 days for debugging and security purposes, then automatically deleted (logs do NOT contain file content)

Important: If you close your browser or your session expires before downloading your files, they become permanently inaccessible because the encryption keys are lost. Files will still be automatically deleted from our servers within 1 hour, but they are already unreadable.

7. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Access: You can request information about what data we hold about you
  • Deletion: Your files are automatically deleted within 24 hours; you can also manually revoke Google access at any time
  • Opt-out: You can choose not to use Google Docs conversion if you prefer not to share your Google account information
  • Data portability: You can download your converted files at any time before they expire
  • Revoke access: You can revoke our access to your Google account at any time via your Google Account settings

To exercise these rights or ask questions about your privacy, contact us at the email address listed in Section 10.
